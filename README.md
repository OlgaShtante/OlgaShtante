#### Strategy
I take a strategy-first approach: analyzing scope and mapping where the risk actually lives. A team never catches every issue, the point is to decide, consciously, which risks the team is comfortable releasing with and which it isn't, so the issues that slip through aren't the ones that matter.

#### Exploration
I explore the product beyond the happy path: where edge cases hide, and where communication gaps and undocumented integrations live. I never leave a question unasked; I keep pushing until the team finds the answer.

#### Quality
 Quality doesn't mean zero issues; we live in the real world. It means knowing your actual risk before you ship, and communicating and escalating when it
  matters.
  
#### Automation
Automation guards what's already proven: mature business cases become automated checks that surface regressions early and allow focusing on what's next to explore.

### 🧭 Principles

- **"100% coverage" is a metric, not a promise.** 
It tells you which lines ran, not whether the product works. Confidence comes from what you assert and where, not from the percentage.
- **"Unit E2E" is an oxymoron.** 
Unit means an isolated component; end-to-end means the full workflow across every integrated layer, and every mock inside an "e2e" test pulls it further from the end-to-end it claims to be.
- **An integration test against an unverified fake tests nothing.**
If you mock the other side, the mock must be backed by a contract. Otherwise you've verified your assumptions, not the integration.
- **Automation doesn't replace testers.**
It frees them to think and explore.
- **If QA is the bottleneck, the process is broken, not the QA.**
Fix the process, not the people.
